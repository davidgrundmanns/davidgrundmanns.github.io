biography.

I am a research associate at the Mannheim Centre for European Social Research and Ph.D. Candidate in Political Science at the Graduate School of Economic and Social Sciences. I am researching judicial politics and interdependent strategies of judicial and political actors. Making use of text as data for further statistical modeling is at the core of my methodological interests.

In project C4 of the Collaborative Research Center SFB 884 "Political Economy of Reforms", we are scaling the German Federal Constitutional Court on a unidimensional latent policy space utilizing Bayesian ideal point estimation.

Furthermore, I am a course instructor at the Chair of Political Science, Quanitative methods in the Social Sciences at the University of Mannheim - teaching undergraduates in “Data Analysis”.
